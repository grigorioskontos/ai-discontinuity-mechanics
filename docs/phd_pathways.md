## 1. 🇫🇷 France, the most direct and fastest routes

### A. Doctoral contracts (École doctorale / Sorbonne / CNRS)

This is baseline, fastest option.
• Standard 3-year PhD funding via doctoral schools
• Often allocated internally or via lab-level funding (CNRS, ANR projects, chairs)
• Timeline: spring → early summer decisions

💡 Strategy for you:
• Position it as “mathematics of instability + ML for transitions in complex systems”
• Anchor strongly in mechanics → generalisation

👉 This is a reliable path.

### B. ANR Project (very strong, fit, competitive)

The Agence Nationale de la Recherche is a key lever.
• Funds 4–5 year research projects
• Includes PhD + postdoc positions
• Strong emphasis on:
• interdisciplinarity
• high-risk/high-reward
• societal relevance

💡 Topic matches perfectly:
• irreversibility, instability
• prediction of abrupt transitions
• applications to economics / biology

📌 Obe strategy:
• Submit a PRC proposal (longer horizon)

### C. CIFRE PhD (industry-linked)

Via Association Nationale de la Recherche et de la Technologie
• PhD funded by a company (~€1800 net/month)
• Strong for:
• economics (banks, risk, finance)
• biotech / health systems
• AI startups

💡 This could be powerful:
“Predicting regime shifts / instability in economic systems”

👉 Think:
